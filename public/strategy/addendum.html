Section 01

Positioning flip — Andy is the product

The offer is no longer systems or outcomes. The offer is the person placing them.

Old frame (v2)

Credo FS is an outcome-led independent advisor for hospitality. Systems and results take the lead.

New frame (v3)

Andy is the product. The offer is peace of mind built on trust, integrity, and hospitality expertise. Systems are the mechanism, not the story.

Evidence from the 20 April call

Trust me, I’m a doctor.— Andy, on how clients should feel
We’re worse than double glazing salesmen.— Andy, on industry reputation
All you need to do is call me.— Andy, on the proof of trust

What this means for copy

  • First-person voice throughout. Andy speaks directly, not “we.”
  • Lead with the relationship, then the mechanism.
  • Kill any framing that implies Credo FS is a card-machine reseller, an EPOS dealer, or a payment broker. Credo FS is Andy; Andy places the right systems.
Section 02

Core promise — peace of mind, two halves

  1. A system that works. Integrated, fit-for-purpose, installed properly. Won’t die mid-service.
  2. One person to call when it doesn’t. Andy picks up. No ticket queue. No “that’s the other company’s problem.”
Non-negotiable
Both halves must appear on the Home page. Absence of either weakens the promise.

Headline differentiator — single-point accountability

Industry norm

“Ping-pong” between card-machine provider and EPOS provider. One blames the other. Owner loses the Friday night.

Andy norm

One number, one person, one call.

Where this lives

Use this contrast explicitly on Home and Consultancy.

Section 03

Competitor contrast — industry skills gap

The gap

The average card-machine or EPOS sales rep understands very little about the other side of the stack. Systems have become complex — integrations, reporting, inventory, reservations, funding. Most reps were trained to sell one box.

Andy’s edge

Twenty years running the sites, then five-plus years across EPOS, card, connectivity, funding, and reservations. Depth the sales-rep average cannot match.

Copy application

  • Consultancy page: “Most people selling you a card machine don’t understand your till. That’s where the problems come from.”
  • About page: Andy’s operator-side background is the reason for the depth.
  • Avoid naming competitors. The contrast is structural, not personal.
Section 04

Brand scope — Credo Financial Services

Old treatment

“Credo UK” in places; naming inconsistent; implied card/EPOS only.

New treatment

Credo Financial Services is the parent identity.

Scope — explicit

  • EPOS + card — core
  • Business funding — Liberus, U-Lend; partner-driven, Andy-placed
  • Reservations — Call Seats, plus integrations to the EPOS
  • Connectivity — Starlink for hospitality, emerging
All of these sit under Andy’s single-point accountability. The name “Credo FS” works across print, podcast, and onboarding.
Section 05

Products direction — Square flagship

Old treatment (v2)

Vendor-neutral products grid. “Brands I trust.”

New treatment (v3)

Square integrated system is the flagship recommendation.

Why Square

  • Integrated till + card processing — one provider, one support line. Reinforces accountability.
  • Customer display screen, not just a terminal. Removes the competitor rep’s entry point (“let me show you a better card reader”).
  • Often no rental fee.
  • Andy is actively moving clients onto it.
Supporting grid

The grid remains — Square is the lead story. Other brands (Teya, Sunmi, Shift4, Loyverse, Kuulseats, Elavon, plus funding partners) support specific fit cases.

If your current setup already does this, I’ll tell you. I don’t push Square when a client doesn’t need to move.— Honesty carve-out (copy line)
Section 06

Audience frame — multi-site reassurance

Old frame

Single-site hospitality independents (Phase 2 interview confirmed this as primary target).

New frame

Primary audience remains single-site independents. But copy must explicitly reassure multi-site operators (3–30 venues) so Andy is credible to that segment without alienating the independents.

Mechanics

  • Add a multi-site reassurance block to the Home page (e.g. feature cards or stats) without making it the lead.
  • Consultancy page mentions multi-site where it fits.
  • Near-term outreach target: Kevin Georgel, St Austell (named in Andy’s LinkedIn network).
Language to use
“From one venue to thirty.”
Not “enterprise.” Not “group.”
